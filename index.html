TL;DR: ViGLUE is a Vietnamese General Language Understanding Evaluation benchmark, including 12 tasks on various domains, and the baseline models finetuned from different language models.

Abstract

As the number of language models has increased, various benchmarks have been suggested to assess the proficiency of the models in natural language understanding. However, there is a lack of such a benchmark in Vietnamese due to the difficulty in accessing natural language processing datasets or the scarcity of task-specific datasets. ViGLUE, the proposed dataset collection, is a Vietnamese General Language Understanding Evaluation benchmark developed using three methods: translating an existing benchmark, generating new corpora, and collecting available datasets. ViGLUE contains twelve tasks and encompasses over ten areas and subjects, enabling it to evaluate models comprehensively over a broad spectrum of aspects. Baseline models utilizing multilingual language models are also provided for all tasks in the proposed benchmarks. In addition, the study of the available Vietnamese large language models is conducted to explore the language models’ ability in the few-shot learning framework, leading to the exploration of the relationship between specific tasks and the number of shots.

Model Analysis

Few-shot learning performance of BLOOM model family with k = 0, 1, 2, 4, denoted by the star, the plus, the circle, and the diamond markers, respectively.
  • Increasing the number of examples (k) benefits models on QNLI across all sizes.
  • CoLA, MNLI, MRPC, and QQP don’t benefit from few-shot learning; k=0 performs best.
  • BLOOM-176B outperforms smaller models in QNLI, RTE, and SST2 but performs similarly to smaller models on other tasks.
  • When the model size increases, the performance of NLI and sentiment tasks improves.
  • Increasing the model size does not always improve the performance on inference tasks (MNLI, QNLI and WMNLI).
Comparing BLOOM-7B1 and PhoGPT on 12 ViGLUE tasks with different k-shot settings.
  • PhoGPT outperforms BLOOM-7B1 on 6/12 tasks, especially in zero-shot learning.
  • BLOOM-7B1 performs better than PhoGPT when examples (k=1, 2, 4) are provided.
  • Zero-shot learning performs best in CoLA, MNLI, MRPC, QQP, and WNLI. Increasing examples in prompts generally decreases performance in those tasks.
  • For QNLI and VNRTE, increasing examples improves performance.
  • Performance for QQP, SST2, VSFC, and VSMEC fluctuates when k changes.
Further results and analysis of multilingual and Vietnamese BERT-based language models can be found in the paper.
